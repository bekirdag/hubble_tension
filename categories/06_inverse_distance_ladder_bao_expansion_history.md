# Inverse Distance Ladder, BAO, and Expansion History

## Solution Idea

This category constrains possible solutions through the distance-redshift relation. BAO calibrates a
standard ruler, supernovae map relative distances, and chronometers estimate H(z). Together they
form inverse-distance-ladder tests that limit late-time fixes and stress-test early-time changes
through consistency with DESI, BOSS/eBOSS, WiggleZ, 6dF, Ly-alpha BAO, and supernova data.

## Main Tradeoff

These papers are often not proposed solutions by themselves; they are the filters that many
solutions must pass. A model that raises H0 but breaks BAO/SN/chronometer consistency is not viable.
