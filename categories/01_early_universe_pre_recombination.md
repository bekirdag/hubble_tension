# Early-Universe and Pre-Recombination Solutions

## Solution Idea

This family tries to solve the tension by changing the early expansion history or microphysics
before the CMB is released. The common target is the sound horizon: if the sound horizon inferred
from early-universe physics is smaller, the same CMB angular scale can allow a larger H0. Mechanisms
in the saved corpus include early dark energy, multi-field/axion-like EDE, dark acoustic
oscillations, recombination-history changes, CMB temperature/polarization checks, BBN consistency
tests, and CMB data sets that constrain these ideas.

## Main Tradeoff

These models are attractive because they attack the CMB side of the problem directly. The cost is
that they must also preserve the detailed CMB damping tail, polarization, lensing, BBN, baryon-
density constraints, and BAO/SN consistency.
